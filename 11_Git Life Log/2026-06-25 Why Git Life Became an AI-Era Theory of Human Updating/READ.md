# Git Life Log

## Why Git Life Became an AI-Era Theory of Human Updating

### Background

The original concept of *Git Life* was simple.

It was a book about applying the philosophy of Git and version control to personal life.

The central ideas were:

- Record your life.
- Preserve your Observation History.
- Do not rush to assign meaning.
- Let meaning emerge through future updates.

At that stage, Git Life was primarily a practical framework for personal reflection.

---

## What Changed

During a series of brainstorming sessions, an unexpected realization emerged.

Git Life is not simply about recording life.

It is becoming a theory about **how humans continue updating themselves in the AI era.**

Several observations led to this shift.

### Observation 1

Modern AI lowers the cost of obtaining knowledge.

The challenge is no longer finding information.

The challenge becomes continuing to update one's own thinking.

### Observation 2

Research on AI "sycophancy" suggests that AI often validates the user's existing beliefs.

An AI that constantly agrees with us may unintentionally slow our ability to update ourselves.

The danger is not misinformation.

The danger is the loss of self-updating.

### Observation 3

The concept of "luck" itself changes in the AI era.

Rather than asking:

> *"Am I lucky?"*

a better question becomes:

> *"Am I continuously creating new connections?"*

Luck increasingly becomes a consequence of observation, curiosity, and connection.

---

## New Understanding

Originally, Git Life focused on recording observations.

Now another layer has become visible.

Observation alone is not the final goal.

The real value lies in recording **how our understanding changes over time.**

The important asset is not knowledge itself.

It is the **difference** between previous and current understanding.

In Git terminology:

- Observation creates History.
- Difference creates Value.
- Updating creates Growth.

---

## Structural Update

The original structure emphasized:

- Observation
- Curiosity
- Meaning
- Recording

The updated structure introduces several new themes.

### New Theme 1

AI makes human self-updating more important than ever.

### New Theme 2

The greatest danger is not AI replacing humans.

It is humans stopping their own updating.

### New Theme 3

Git Life is becoming less a self-help book and more a theory of continuous human updating.

### New Theme 4

"Diff" becomes a core concept.

Rather than asking:

> *"What did I learn?"*

Git Life increasingly asks:

> *"What changed in my understanding?"*

## Learning Note

This brainstorming revealed an important shift.

Git Life is no longer simply a method for keeping personal records.

It is gradually becoming a framework for understanding how humans continue to evolve in an age where AI can both accelerate learning and unintentionally discourage self-updating.

The core concept has also become clearer.

The value of a life is not the amount of knowledge accumulated.

The value lies in the continuous record of **how one's understanding changes over time.**

The most important structural change during this brainstorming was the emergence of **Diff** as a central concept.

Until now, Observation History had been the core idea.

Now it has become clear that Observation is the input, while **Diff** is where the true value is created.

This mirrors Git itself.

Git is valuable not because it merely stores history, but because it makes visible **what has changed**.

Git Life is evolving in the same direction.

It is becoming a framework for recording not only life itself, but the continuous differences that shape human growth.
