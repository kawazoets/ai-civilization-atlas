# AI Capitalism

## Current Definition

AI Capitalism describes a capital allocation environment in which AI-mediated observation, evaluation, and prediction increasingly influence the flow of capital.

---

## Why This Concept Was Needed

Traditional economic frameworks increasingly failed to explain what I was observing.

Profitable companies were being ignored.

Loss-making companies were receiving enormous valuations.

Markets appeared to react before information became widely visible.

Something important was occurring beneath traditional financial indicators.

---

## Personal Origin

I did not arrive at AI Capitalism by studying AI.

I arrived at it by failing to explain the world through existing frameworks.

At first, I assumed profits remained the primary driver of value.

That explanation became increasingly difficult to defend.

I then explored market psychology, macroeconomics, technology cycles, and platform economics.

Each explained part of the picture.

None explained the whole.

The turning point came when I began viewing AI not as a tool, but as an observation environment.

## Key Realization

The question was never whether AI would replace humans.

The more important question was:

Who observes?

Who evaluates?

Who allocates capital?

As these functions increasingly become machine-mediated, the structure of capitalism itself begins to change.

---

## Discarded Explanations

### AI as Productivity Tool

Rejected.

This explained efficiency gains but not capital allocation behavior.

### AI as Automation

Rejected.

Automation alone could not explain market valuation shifts.

### AI as AGI Narrative

Rejected.

The observed changes were already occurring without AGI.
