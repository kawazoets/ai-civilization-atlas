# Factor Log

Factor Log records the underlying factors that led to the emergence of specific ideas, frameworks, and theories.

Unlike Research Logs, which document observations, experiences, and changes in understanding, Factor Logs focus on causality.

The purpose is not to explain what a concept is, but to examine why it emerged.

Questions addressed in this section include:

- Why did a particular idea emerge?
- What observations contributed to it?
- Which assumptions were rejected?
- What experiences changed the direction of thinking?
- Which factors proved decisive?

This section serves as a record of intellectual causality.

Knowledge can be reproduced.

Conclusions can be copied.

However, the path that led to a conclusion is often unique.

Factor Log attempts to preserve that path.

---

## Difference from Research Log

### Research Log

Research Logs document observations and learning.

Examples:

- Why FC-BGA Changed My View of AI
- Why Factory Control Systems Changed My View of AI
- Why Broadcom Led Me to Infrastructure Thinking

Focus:

"What did I observe?"

"What changed my understanding?"

---

### Factor Log

Factor Logs document the causes behind ideas.

Examples:

- Why AI-Scored Society Emerged
- Why AI Capitalism Emerged
- Why AI Civilization Atlas Emerged

Focus:

"Why did this idea emerge?"

"What factors produced it?"
