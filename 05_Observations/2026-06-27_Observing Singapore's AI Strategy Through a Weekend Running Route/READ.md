# Observing Singapore's AI Strategy Through a Weekend Running Route

On Saturday morning, I went for a run around the Pasir Ris Wafer Fab Park area.

Pasir Ris Wafer Fab Park is an industrial park project developed and managed by JTC in Singapore.

My original purpose was simply to visit the area around Applied Materials after reading recent news about its expansion project. However, the run led to far more discoveries than I had expected.

## ① UMC

The UMC facility appeared to occupy a site roughly three times larger than the Broadcom-related substrate factory where I currently work.

Seeing the site firsthand made it clear that front-end semiconductor manufacturing (Foundry) requires facilities and buildings on a completely different scale from back-end packaging (FC-BGA).

## ② Soitec Next to UMC

At first, I assumed that Soitec was simply located next to UMC.

However, I noticed that the two buildings were physically connected at the rear, forming what appeared to be a single integrated structure.

After checking Google Maps imagery taken during the construction phase, I confirmed that this connection was part of the original design rather than a later extension.

At present, the reason for this architectural connection remains unknown. However, considering that Soitec is a semiconductor wafer material supplier, the structure suggests a close supply-chain relationship. Nevertheless, no public information explaining the purpose of the building connection could be found.

## ③ SSMC Across from UMC

I also learned that SSMC is a joint venture between TSMC and NXP.

NXP is a Dutch semiconductor company and one of the world's leading manufacturers of automotive semiconductors.

This reminded me that Singapore's semiconductor industry is supported not only by Taiwanese companies but also by significant European, particularly Dutch, investment.

Another realization came during this walk.

I learned that companies such as UMC, Soitec, Micron, Jabil, and SSMC are major tenants within the Wafer Fab Parks developed and managed by Singapore's government agency, JTC.

Unlike Applied Materials or Advanced Substrate Technologies, these companies operate under a different land development scheme as part of an industrial cluster established by JTC.

I also noticed that PUB facilities are located around Pasir Ris Wafer Fab Park.

This helped me understand that water infrastructure itself is one of the essential components supporting this semiconductor cluster, alongside the manufacturing facilities.

What impressed me most during today's walk was how my perspective changed.

Previously, I had viewed AI-related companies individually.

By the end of the run, I found myself looking at the entire national industrial infrastructure designed to support them.

# Learning Note

## SSMC / NXP

- SSMC is a joint venture between TSMC and NXP.
- NXP is a Dutch company (formerly Philips Semiconductor) and one of the world's leading manufacturers of automotive semiconductors.
- Next, I plan to review NXP's financial statements.

## Soitec

- Soitec is not a semiconductor manufacturer but a supplier of engineered semiconductor wafers, including SOI wafers.
- SOI (Silicon-on-Insulator) wafers are specialized silicon wafers with an insulating layer that provides higher speed, lower power consumption, and improved noise resistance.
- RF-SOI wafers are widely used in smartphones, 5G base stations, Wi-Fi, and other radio-frequency applications.
- Soitec is one of the world's leading manufacturers of SOI wafers.
- UMC and Soitec are not competitors. Soitec is an important supplier of SOI wafers and other engineered wafers to UMC.
- UMC awarded Soitec the Outstanding Supplier Award in 2021.
- The two companies also collaborate on RF-SOI technologies.
- The buildings of UMC and Soitec were designed as an integrated structure from the beginning of construction.
- No public information explaining the purpose of the building connection could be found.

## UMC

- UMC is a semiconductor foundry that manufactures chips designed by its customers rather than producing its own semiconductor products.
- UMC's new Singapore fab officially opened in April 2025.
- The new fab focuses primarily on 22nm and 28nm process technologies.

## Foundry

- Front-end semiconductor fabs are significantly larger than back-end packaging facilities such as FC-BGA plants.
- This is because they require numerous process tools, including lithography, deposition, etching, CMP, ion implantation, and cleaning equipment.
- They also require massive utility infrastructure such as ultrapure water systems, chemical supply systems, exhaust systems, gas supply systems, and electrical substations.
- Semiconductor manufacturing capacity is commonly expressed as Wafer Starts Per Month (WSPM).

## JTC

- Wafer Fab Park is an industrial park developed by JTC.
- Companies such as UMC, Soitec, Micron, and Jabil operate under JTC's land lease scheme.
- This land development model differs from that of Advanced Substrate Technologies (AST).

## PUB

- PUB facilities are located around Wafer Fab Park.
- Semiconductor manufacturing requires enormous amounts of ultrapure and industrial water, making water infrastructure one of the fundamental elements supporting the semiconductor industry.
- Lorong Halus Wetland is part of the water management infrastructure associated with PUB.

## Pasir Ris Wafer Fab Park

- Pasir Ris Wafer Fab Park is still under continuous development and expansion.
- The park is designed not only with factories but also with integrated infrastructure including roads, water systems, electricity, and utilities.
