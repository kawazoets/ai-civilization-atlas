Why I Started Thinking About Connecting Assets to AI-Driven Value

Observation

Today, I watched a discussion about Permanent Underclass in the AI era.

At first, I thought the discussion was about access to advanced AI models. The argument seemed straightforward: those who could use the latest AI would gain an advantage over those who could not.

However, as the discussion continued, I realized the focus had quietly shifted.

The real topic was no longer AI usage.

It was ownership.

The examples increasingly focused on early employees of AI companies, stock options, IPOs, and private shares becoming liquid assets. The conclusion was not simply that people should use AI more effectively. It was that people who secured ownership of assets whose value would rise because of AI would benefit the most.

This made me reconsider the relationship between AI and capital.

Recognition Update

I began asking a different question.

Instead of asking,

"How can I use AI better?"

I found myself asking,

"What kinds of assets will AI make more valuable?"

This felt like a much more fundamental question.

Using AI may improve productivity.

Owning assets whose value increases because AI becomes more widely adopted is a different mechanism entirely.

The two should not be confused.

A Connection to Singapore

This also changed how I viewed Singapore.

Singapore has never tried to own everything.

Instead, it seems to focus on occupying positions where value naturally flows.

Ports.

Finance.

Semiconductor manufacturing.

Advanced packaging.

Data centers.

Regional infrastructure.

Even projects such as DayOne extend beyond Singapore's physical borders by connecting land, energy, and infrastructure across neighboring countries.

The objective does not appear to be ownership of every physical asset.

Rather, it is participation in the value created by connecting those assets.

Extending the Idea to Individuals

This led me to another question.

Can individuals adopt the same strategy?

Perhaps the goal is not simply to become better AI users.

Perhaps it is to build unique assets that can connect to the value AI creates.

These assets could take many forms:

technical expertise
professional reputation
educational achievements
intellectual property
trusted networks
businesses
long-term research

The common characteristic is not that they exist.

It is that they can participate in AI-driven value creation.

Current Hypothesis

My current thinking is still incomplete, but today's discussion led me to a new working hypothesis.

The question in the AI era may not simply be:

How do I use AI?

It may become:

How do I position my own assets so that they become connected to the value that AI continuously creates?

I think this question deserves much deeper exploration.

Learning Note

Today's discussion helped me distinguish three different ideas that are often treated as the same:

Using AI.
Owning assets whose value increases because of AI.
Positioning those assets where AI continuously amplifies their value.

The third idea feels particularly important.

Rather than thinking only about ownership, I want to explore how value-creating connections become a form of capital in the AI era.
